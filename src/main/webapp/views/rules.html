Canasta Rules

Overview

Canasta is a type of rummy played with 4, 6, or 8 players in teams of two. Each team is trying to reach a total score of 5,000 before the opposing team(s).


Card Types

  • Wilds(2s and Jokers)

    Can be assigned any desired rank by the player and when discarded, prevent the next player from drawing from the discard pile like Black 3s

  • Black 3s

    When discarded, prevent the next player from drawing from the discard pile

  • Red 3s

    Yield an immediate 100 bonus points to whoever draws them

  • Naturals

    4s - Kings


Card Values

  • 2s : 20 points
  • Black 3s : 5 points
  • Red 3s : 100 points
  • 4s - 7s : 5 points
  • 8s - Kings : 10 points
  • Aces : 20 points
  • Jokers : 50 points

The Deal

11 cards are dealt to each players starting with the player to the left of the dealer clockwise. The remaining cards are placed in the middle to seve as the stock. The top card of the stock is flipped over and placed by its side to start a discard pile. If this card is a 3, 2, or Joker another card is flipped until a valid card is reached.

Red 3s

Whenever a red 3 is dealt or drawn it is immediately played face up in front of the player and a replacement is drawn. Each red 3 earns a team 100 points. If all 4 are aquired in a single round an additional 400 points are rewarded for a total of 800 points.


The Play

The player to the left of the dealer begins. Play consists of drawing, melding, and then discarding.

Drawing

Players must draw from either the stock or if possible the discard pile. Only the top card of the discard is immediately accessible. If the top card is a Black 3 or Wild then the discard is frozen for that turn and can not be drawn from. Otherwise if the player wishes to draw from the discard they must use it in a valid meld. At that point the entirety of the discard pile is put into the players hand

Melding

The melding phase of the turn is optional. If a team has not melded yet in that round they must meet an initial meld point criteria depending on their total score. A valid meld consists of at least 3 cards of the same rank. Wild cards can assume any rank the player desires; however, They can not be reassigned after being used in a meld. Melds must have more natural cards than wild cards A player can add to existing melds of theirs or their partner's. A meld of 7 or more cards forms a Canasta. Canastas are considered natural if they contain no wild cards and unnatural otherwise. Natural Canastas and Unnatural Canastas are worth 500 points and 300 points respectively.

Initial Meld Point Criteria:
  • less than 0 : 0 points
  • less than 1,500 : 50 points
  • less than 3,000 : 90 points
  • 3,000 or more : 120 points

Discard

At the end of the turn a player must choose one card from their hand to discard. Black 3s and Wilds freeze the discard pile for the next player.

Going Out

The round ends when one player gets rid of all the cards in their hand. A player is not allowed to go out until their team has made at least one Canasta. A player receives a bonus of 100 points for going out and the round ends.

Scoring

  • Going Out : 100 points
  • Natural Canasta : 500 points
  • Unnatural Canasta : 300 points
  • Red 3s : 100 points
  • All 4 Red 3s Bonus : 400 points
  • Card Values of Each Card in a Meld

Winning The Game

First team to have accumalated 5,000 or more total points at the end of a round wins. If two or more teams both surpass this score than the higher score wins.
